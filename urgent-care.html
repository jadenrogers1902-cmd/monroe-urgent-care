Who it is for

For patients who need care today, but not an emergency room.

Urgent care is for children, adults, families, and workers with non-life-threatening symptoms, minor injuries, or testing needs that should not wait for a primary-care appointment.

Walk-ins and appointments

Visit the way that works for you.

Walk-ins are welcome daily, and online booking is available when you want to reserve a spot before you arrive.

What we treat

Illnesses, minor injuries, and common testing needs.

Illnesses we treat

  • Cold and flu symptoms
  • Sore throat and strep concerns
  • Ear and eye infections
  • UTI symptoms
  • Rashes and skin concerns

Injuries we treat

  • Sprains and strains
  • Minor cuts and burns
  • Work-related minor injuries
  • Everyday aches and minor trauma

Testing available

  • Flu, COVID, RSV, and strep testing
  • Pregnancy testing
  • Urinalysis
  • Drug screens during posted hours

What to expect during your visit

Check in with the front desk, share your symptoms or visit reason, complete any needed testing, and meet with a care team member for next steps.

What to bring

  • Photo ID
  • Insurance card, if using insurance
  • Payment method for copay or self-pay
  • Medication list and relevant medical history
  • Required forms for physicals or workplace visits

Insurance and self-pay

Most insurance is accepted, and self-pay is available for patients without insurance. Ask the clinic about payment expectations before or during check-in.

When to go to the ER

If you are experiencing chest pain, trouble breathing, severe bleeding, stroke symptoms, loss of consciousness, major trauma, or another life-threatening emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.
