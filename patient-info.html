During your visit

What to expect

Check in

Share your visit reason and provide ID, insurance, payment, and any required forms.

Testing information

Testing may include flu, COVID, RSV, strep, pregnancy testing, urinalysis, drug screens, or other testing as available.

Physicals information

Bring any required forms for school, sports, work, DOT, or pre-employment physicals.

Emergency guidance

If you are experiencing chest pain, trouble breathing, severe bleeding, stroke symptoms, loss of consciousness, major trauma, or another life-threatening emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

FAQ

Common patient questions

Do I need an appointment?

No. Walk-ins are welcome, and online booking is available.

Do you accept insurance?

Many insurance plans are accepted. Bring your insurance card and call with billing questions.

Can I be seen without insurance?

Yes. Self-pay options are available.

Can Monroe Urgent Care treat emergencies?

Urgent care is for non-life-threatening needs. Call 911 or go to the ER for life-threatening symptoms.

What should I bring for a physical?

Bring a photo ID, the required form, glasses or hearing aids if used, and a medication list.
