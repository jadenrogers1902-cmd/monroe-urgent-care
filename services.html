Who services are for

Families, workers, employers, and self-pay patients.

Monroe Urgent Care supports everyday medical visits for local patients as well as practical occupational health services for area employers.

What to expect

Clear options without a complicated path.

Choose the service that fits your need, bring the basics for check-in, and the team will guide next steps for care, testing, forms, or follow-up.

What Monroe provides

Core services

Urgent Care

Same-day care for non-life-threatening illnesses and minor injuries.

Testing & Diagnostics

Flu, COVID, RSV, strep, pregnancy, urinalysis, and other testing as available.

Physicals

School, sports, work, pre-employment, and DOT physicals.

Occupational Health

Drug screens, workplace testing, physicals, and employer support.

Workers' Compensation

Support for work-related injuries and employer documentation needs.

IV Hydration

Wellness-focused hydration services through Monroe IV Drip.

What to bring

  • Photo ID
  • Insurance card, if using insurance
  • Payment method
  • Any service-specific forms
  • Medication list if relevant

Insurance and self-pay

Most insurance is accepted, and self-pay is available. Service costs and requirements can vary by visit type.

Emergency guidance

If you are experiencing chest pain, trouble breathing, severe bleeding, stroke symptoms, loss of consciousness, major trauma, or another life-threatening emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.
